

# CIA Triad

<p align="center">
  <img src="images/cia.jpg" alt="CIA Diagram" width="700">
</p>

The **CIA Triad** stands for **Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability**.

It is one of the most fundamental concepts in cybersecurity and serves as a foundational security model upon which security policies, controls, and defensive mechanisms are built.

Think of the CIA Triad as the three primary goals of information security:

* **C** → Confidentiality
* **I** → Integrity
* **A** → Availability

---

## 1. Confidentiality

### Definition

Confidentiality ensures that information or data is accessible only to authorized individuals and is protected from unauthorized access.

### Goal

To prevent unauthorized disclosure of information.

### Example

Imagine a university that stores sensitive information such as:

* Student records
* Academic records
* Examination scores and results
* Personal information

This data should only be accessible to authorized users such as students, teachers, principals, administrators, and other approved personnel.

If an outsider or attacker gains unauthorized access to this information, **confidentiality is compromised**.

---

## 2. Integrity

### Definition

Integrity ensures that data remains accurate, authentic, consistent, and unaltered unless modified by an authorized entity.

### Goal

To prevent unauthorized modification, corruption, or malicious tampering of data.

### Example

Suppose a student scores **90 out of 100** on an examination.

If an attacker changes the score from **90** to **32**, the data has been altered without authorization.

In this case, **integrity is compromised** because the information is no longer accurate or trustworthy.

---

## 3. Availability

### Definition

Availability ensures that data, systems, and services are accessible to authorized users whenever they are needed.

### Goal

To prevent disruptions, downtime, or failures that make systems or information unavailable.

### Example

Imagine you own a house and have the right to enter it whenever you want.

You possess the key to your house, but one day the key is lost.

Although the house and everything inside it remain safe, you can no longer access it.

In this scenario:

* The contents of the house remain protected (**Confidentiality is maintained**).
* However, you cannot access your own property (**Availability is compromised**).

This example demonstrates that data can remain secure while still being unavailable to authorized users.

---

# Summary

The CIA Triad consists of three core principles of cybersecurity:

| Principle       | Purpose                                                    |
| --------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| Confidentiality | Prevent unauthorized access to information                 |
| Integrity       | Prevent unauthorized modification of information           |
| Availability    | Ensure authorized users can access information when needed |

A secure system should maintain all three principles simultaneously. If any one of them is compromised, the overall security of the system is weakened.
