# Principle of Least Privilege (PoLP)



## Definition



The Principle of Least Privilege (PoLP) states that a system, user, application, process, or device must be given only the minimum permissions that are required to perform its task. They should not be given more privileges, more permissions, or more access than what is actually needed.



The only motive behind this principle is to minimize the attack surface for an attacker and help strengthen the overall security of the organization.



---



## Example



Suppose a new employee joins a company as a Finance Manager.



The employee should only be given access to the financial records that are required to perform their job. They should not be given access to the entire company database, which may contain sensitive information such as employee personal details, account numbers, bank details, or other confidential data.



Giving the employee only the required access allows them to perform their work comfortably while also protecting sensitive information from unnecessary exposure.



---



## Why is the Principle of Least Privilege Important



- Minimizes the attack surface.

- Reduces the impact of compromised accounts.

- Prevents unauthorized access to sensitive information.

- Limits insider threats.

- Helps maintain confidentiality, integrity, and availability (CIA Triad).

- Makes privilege escalation attacks more difficult.

- Helps organizations comply with security standards and regulations.



---



## Key Points



- Give only the permissions that are required.

- Never grant unnecessary access.

- Review and remove unused privileges regularly.

- Apply the principle to users, applications, services, devices, and processes.
